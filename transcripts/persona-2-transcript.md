### Interpretation

This employee framed today through an **external locus lens**, attributing frustration to circumstances outside their control and initially deferring to others to solve problems. When asked about contribution, they immediately identified a moment of feeling overlooked rather than a moment of giving, suggesting an **entitlement-oriented focus** — their attention was on what they didn't receive rather than what they did give. Finally, when reflecting on the day's challenge, they centered entirely on themselves, exhibiting a **self-focused (self-centric) perspective**.

**Reflection:** This pattern suggests someone who may be experiencing burnout or disconnection. The external locus isn't permanent failure — it's a signal. On tough days, people naturally externalize. But when combined with entitlement focus and self-centeredness, it indicates the person is stuck in a defensive crouch.

**Possible interpretation:** This person gave something today (externally-caused frustration wouldn't happen if they weren't doing their job) but didn't recognize it. The tree's reflection is an invitation: "What did you do that wouldn't have happened without you?"

**What would help:** Reconnecting to their agency (small wins), noticing contribution (reflecting on helping), and widening perspective (remembering who benefits from their work).
